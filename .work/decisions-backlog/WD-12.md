## Summary

Advanced encrypted query capabilities and the client-side encryption SDK.
Covers prefix/wildcard queries on DET-encrypted fields, fuzzy matching via
locality-sensitive hashing, cross-field encrypted joins, and the external
SDK for caller-managed encryption. All four were deferred pending encryption
key rotation and per-field key binding — both now confirmed and specified
in encryption.primitives-lifecycle.

Decisions in this cluster: encrypted-prefix-wildcard-queries,
encrypted-fuzzy-matching, encrypted-cross-field-joins, client-side-encryption-sdk

## Implementation Notes

**Effort classification:**
- Minor features: client-side-encryption-sdk (API surface design, no crypto research)
- Full features: encrypted-prefix-wildcard-queries, encrypted-fuzzy-matching,
  encrypted-cross-field-joins

**Approach:** client-side-encryption-sdk first — it's API design informed by
encryption.primitives-lifecycle's per-field pre-encryption (R1-R8) and key derivation (R9-R16c), no new
crypto research needed. Then encrypted-prefix-wildcard-queries — needs
research on searchable encryption (prefix tokenization + DET). encrypted-fuzzy-matching
builds on similar infrastructure (LSH + Bloom filter over DET tokens) and
should be evaluated after prefix queries. encrypted-cross-field-joins is the
most complex — depends on distributed-join-execution (query.distributed-join-strategy) and has significant
leakage implications; needs careful threat model analysis.

**Internal dependency:** encrypted-fuzzy-matching depends on
encrypted-prefix-wildcard-queries being evaluated first (shared infrastructure).

**Key specs to reference:** encryption.primitives-lifecycle (Encryption Lifecycle), query.distributed-join-strategy (Distributed
Join Strategy), encryption.primitives-variants (Field-Level In-Memory Encryption)
