## Summary

Operational safety during rebalancing and node failure recovery. Covers
partition takeover ordering, WAL replay throttling, in-flight write
protection, un-WAL'd memtable data loss prevention, and corruption repair
strategies. All five decisions were deferred pending WD-07 (Partitioning &
Rebalancing) and partition replication (F32) — both now specified.

Decisions in this cluster: partition-takeover-priority,
concurrent-wal-replay-throttling, in-flight-write-protection,
un-walled-memtable-data-loss, corruption-repair-recovery

## Implementation Notes

**Effort classification:**
- Minor features: partition-takeover-priority, concurrent-wal-replay-throttling
- Full features: in-flight-write-protection, un-walled-memtable-data-loss,
  corruption-repair-recovery

**Approach:** Minor features first (takeover ordering, replay throttling).
in-flight-write-protection and un-walled-memtable-data-loss are closely
related — both concern data loss during ownership transitions, and both
depend on replication (F32). corruption-repair-recovery is the most complex
— needs research on read repair, anti-entropy, and targeted replica fetch
strategies before /architect.

**Key specs to reference:** F27 (Rebalancing Safety), F29 (Rebalancing
Operations), F32 (Partition Replication)
